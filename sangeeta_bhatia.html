Dr. Sangeeta Bhatia

"My dad brought me to see the lab, and it really captured my imagination. I grabbed onto the idea that instrumentation and devices could be useful for human health."

Sangeeta Bhatia received her undergraduate degree in Biomedical Engineering at Brown University and then went on to get a M.S. at MIT and Ph.D., and M.D. through a joint program between MIT and Harvard University. She is currently a Health Sciences and Technology, Electrical Engineering and Computer Science professor at MIT. Her main scientific accomplishment is pioneering technology that interfaces living cells with synthetic systems, enabling new applications in tissue regeneration, stem cell differentiation, medical diagnostics and drug delivery.

Interesting Facts

  1. Her family wanted her to be a doctor, engineer, or entrepreneur when she grew up. She ultimately became all three.
  2. She took a gap year after getting her undergraduate degree to find out if she wanted to work in the medical industry or academics.
  3. She is an advocate for gender diversity and youth involvement in STEM
  4. She is a co-author on the first undergraduate textbook on tissue engineering.
